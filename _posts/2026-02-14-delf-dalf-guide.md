## üéØ DELF B2 & Campus France: The Magic Level

Most French public universities process international applications via **Campus France**, a government agency.

* **DELF (A1 - B2):** General French proficiency.
* **DALF (C1 - C2):** Advanced Academic French (Near-native fluency).

**Do you need C1?**
Usually, no. Only if you are studying heavy reading-based subjects like Law, Psychology, or Literature. For Engineering, IT, or Business Management, B2 is the standard entry requirement.

## üö´ Who Should NOT Take the DELF?

While the DELF is powerful, it is not for everyone. Do not take this exam if:

* ‚ùå **You are applying exclusively to English-taught programs:** Hundreds of programs in France are taught entirely in English (like at HEC or INSEAD). These require IELTS, not DELF.
* ‚ùå **You are targeting Canadian PR:** Canada's Express Entry system requires the TEF or TCF, not the DELF.
* ‚ùå **You cannot commit the time:** Reaching B2 requires roughly 600 hours of study. It is a marathon, not a sprint.
