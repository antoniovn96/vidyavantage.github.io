<h2 id="b2-requirement">üéØ DELF B2 & Campus France: The Magic Level</h2>
<p>Most French public universities process international applications via <strong>Campus France</strong>, a government agency.</p> 

<div class="magic-box">
  <h2 style="margin-top:0; color: #0A2342; border: none; font-size: 1.8rem;">üéì The B2 Requirement</h2>
  <p style="font-size: 1.1rem; color: #334155; margin-bottom: 0;">If you want to do a Bachelors or Masters taught entirely in <strong>French</strong>, Campus France and the universities strictly require a <strong>DELF B2</strong> diploma.</p>
</div>

<ul>
  <li><strong>DELF (A1 - B2):</strong> General French proficiency.</li>
  <li><strong>DALF (C1 - C2):</strong> Advanced Academic French (Near-native fluency).</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Do you need C1?</strong><br>
Usually, no. Only if you are studying heavy reading-based subjects like Law, Psychology, or Literature. For Engineering, IT, or Business Management, B2 is the standard entry requirement.</p>
