* **DELF (A1 - B2):** General French.
* **DALF (C1 - C2):** Advanced Academic French (Near-native fluency).

**Do you need C1?**
Only if you are studying Law, Psychology, or Literature. For Engineering/Management, B2 is usually enough.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è DELF vs TEF (Don't Confuse Them!)

This is the most common mistake Indian students make.

1.  **DELF/DALF:** For **Studying** in France. (Academic, Lifetime Validity).
2.  **TEF Canada:** For **Immigrating** to Canada (PR Visa, 2-Year Validity).

**Scenario:**
* *Applying for Masters in Paris?* Take **DELF**.
* *Applying for Express Entry Canada?* Take **TEF**.
