<h2 id="coaching-myth">The "Coaching Mafia" Myth</h2>
<p>Every year, Indian students spend crores on IELTS coaching.</p>
<p><strong>Here is the truth:</strong> IELTS is not a test of your <em>intelligence</em> (like JEE). It is a test of your <em>familiarity</em> with English.</p>

<p>If you watch Netflix without subtitles and can write a decent email, you can score Band 8 <strong>on your own</strong>. You just need a strategy.</p>

<h2 id="4-week-plan">üöÄ The 4-Week Battle Plan (Module by Module)</h2>
<p>To get an overall Band 8, you need to master all 4 sections.</p>

<div class="module-grid">
  <div class="module-card">
    <span class="band-score">üëÇ Listening</span>
    <p><strong>The Trap:</strong> Losing focus for 5 seconds misses 3 answers.</p>
    <p><strong>The Hack:</strong> Read the questions <em>before</em> the audio starts. Predict the answers (e.g., "This blank needs a Number").</p>
  </div>

  <div class="module-card">
    <span class="band-score">üìñ Reading</span>
    <p><strong>The Trap:</strong> Reading the whole passage.</p>
    <p><strong>The Hack:</strong> Skim & Scan. Read the first and last sentence of every paragraph to get the "Gist".</p>
  </div>

  <div class="module-card">
    <span class="band-score">‚úçÔ∏è Writing</span>
    <p><strong>The Trap:</strong> Using fancy vocabulary incorrectly.</p>
    <p><strong>The Hack:</strong> Structure is King. Use the 4-paragraph structure: Intro -> Body 1 -> Body 2 -> Conclusion.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="module-card">
    <span class="band-score">üó£Ô∏è Speaking</span>
    <p><strong>The Trap:</strong> Trying to fake a British/American accent.</p>
    <p><strong>The Hack:</strong> Be natural. Speak fluently. If you make a mistake, correct it and move on. Don't pause.</p>
  </div>
</div>


<h2 id="common-mistakes">‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes that Kill Your Score</h2>
<ol>
  <li><strong>Leaving blanks empty:</strong> There is NO negative marking in IELTS. Guess if you don't know!</li>
  <li><strong>Writing too little:</strong> If Task 2 asks for 250 words, writing 240 will lower your score penalty. Aim for 260-280.</li>
  <li><strong>Memorizing answers:</strong> Examiners are trained to spot "scripted" answers in the Speaking test.</li>
</ol>
