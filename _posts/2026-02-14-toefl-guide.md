<h2 id="marathon">The "Marathon" is Over</h2>
<p>For years, students hated the TOEFL because it was a 3-hour torture session.</p>
<p><strong>Good News:</strong> In 2026, the TOEFL iBT is now <strong>less than 2 hours</strong>. It is officially the shortest English proficiency test.</p>

<p>If you have a short attention span, the TOEFL might now be better for you than the IELTS.</p>

<h2 id="new-format">üìâ The New Format (2026 Update)</h2>
<p>ETS (the creator) has removed all the "filler" questions.</p>

<ol>
  <li><strong>Reading (35 mins):</strong> Only 2 passages now. Fast-paced.</li>
  <li><strong>Listening (36 mins):</strong> 3 Lectures, 2 Conversations.</li>
  <li><strong>Speaking (16 mins):</strong> 4 Tasks. You speak to a screen.</li>
  <li><strong>Writing (29 mins):</strong> <strong>MAJOR CHANGE HERE.</strong></li>
</ol>

<div class="new-feature">
  <h3 style="margin-top:0; color: #b45309; font-size: 1.1rem; border: none;">üö® NEW TASK ALERT: "Writing for an Academic Discussion"</h3>
  <p style="color: #78350f;">The old "Independent Essay" is GONE.</p>
  <p style="margin: 0; color: #78350f;">Now, you must read a professor's question and two student responses, then write your own contribution in <strong>10 minutes</strong>. It mimics a real online college forum.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="speaking-hack">üí° How to Hack the "Speaking" Section</h2>
<p>The Speaking section is where Indians lose the most marks. Not because of accent, but because of <strong>"Umm... Ahh..."</strong></p>

<p>The computer grader hates silence.</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Strategy:</strong> Use templates.</li>
  <li><em>Example:</em> "I believe the professor's argument is valid for two reasons. First, he mentions [Point 1]. Second, he explains [Point 2]."</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Don't try to be creative.</strong> Be robotic, clear, and loud.</p>
