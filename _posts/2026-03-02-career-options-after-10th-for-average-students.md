<h2 id="faq">‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3 style="color: #0A2342; font-size: 1.2rem;">Can average students succeed in life?</h3>
<p>Absolutely. Academic marks only test your ability to memorize and write exams. Many successful entrepreneurs, designers, and corporate professionals were average students academically. Success is built on skills, consistency, and networking.</p>

<h3 style="color: #0A2342; font-size: 1.2rem;">Which stream is easiest after 10th?</h3>
<p>There is no universally "easy" stream. Arts and Commerce have less intense mathematics than Science, but their professional fields (like CA or Law) are highly rigorous. The easiest stream is the one that aligns with your natural interests.</p>

<h3 style="color: #0A2342; font-size: 1.2rem;">Is a diploma better than 11th & 12th?</h3>
<p>For practical learners who want to enter the workforce quickly, diplomas provide faster, focused career entry without the stress of theoretical board exams.</p>

<h3 style="color: #0A2342; font-size: 1.2rem;">What if I don‚Äôt like studying at all?</h3>
<p>If you dislike traditional studying, immediately focus on skill-oriented or vocational courses like Graphic Design, Video Editing, Culinary Arts, or Digital Marketing. Build a portfolio instead of a report card.</p>
